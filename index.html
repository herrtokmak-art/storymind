Memory (builder · query · ranker)

Memory Condenser

Summarizes a character's oldest, low-importance memories into compact recollections once they pile up — bounds growth over long sessions and sharpens retrieval. Uses the Memory model above.
Enable condenser
Two-tier recall. Each turn the app injects a few recent (sharp, verbatim) memories and a few distant (condensed, fuzzier) ones under separate headings. Condensed memories become the distant pool.

Living Universe (gossip)

When characters are present but not in your conversation, they catch a vague impression of who you were with and how it looked. At End Day, charged glimpses (flirtation, arguments, secrecy) can travel through the relationship graph to people who'd care — planted as a vague rumor and an open suspicion in their head, which colors your next scene with them. Most glimpses spread to no one.
Enable gossip
Gossip propagation prompt
Decides who tells whom, and as what. Returns JSON. Use {{user}}, {{witness}}, {{impression}}, {{charge}}, {{location}}, {{witness_ties}}, {{candidates}}.
Confrontations (gossip → someone comes to settle it)
When a character holds a strong, unresolved suspicion about you, they may arrive to confront you. The Scene Writer runs it as a winnable social check: a hidden conviction the evidence sets a floor on, that your excuses, consistency, and honesty move — while their personality decides how movable they are.
Confrontation judge prompt
The unbiased referee — scores your last turn into a conviction delta (it writes no dialogue, decides no ending). Returns JSON. Use {{user}}, {{accuser}}, {{intent}}, {{evidence}}, {{accuser_nature}}, {{conviction}}, {{floor}}, {{ceiling}}, {{player_turn}}.
Offstage agency (characters form grudges/ambitions & act on them)
Characters who are wronged, tempted, or see an opening form private motives — a grievance, ambition, scheme — and pursue them offstage between days: festering, recruiting allies who share the sentiment. When one is ready, it comes back as a confrontation. You insult someone; days later they return, and not always alone.
Intent formation prompt
At End Day, decides whether a character now holds a charged motive. Returns JSON. Use {{user}}, {{char}}, {{nature}}, {{day_memories}}, {{roster}}.
Intent tick prompt (fester & recruit)
Each End Day, develops a live motive. Returns JSON. Use {{holder}}, {{nature}}, {{kind}}, {{target}}, {{aim}}, {{trigger}}, {{strength}}, {{allies}}, {{candidates}}.

Authoring model (world & prompt building)

The strong model used for the rare, high-leverage generation jobs that shape everything downstream: building universes, writing character bios, authoring each world's director notes, and tuning the Living-Universe prompts to fit a world. It runs only a handful of times per universe — not per message — so a top-tier model here is cheap and pays off across the whole playthrough. A capable model is strongly recommended.
